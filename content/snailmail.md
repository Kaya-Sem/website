True friendships are worth their weight in gold. Just like in romantic relationships, it is easy to take things for granted and stop putting in as much effort. Friendships and relationships require regular maintenance and upkeep. It is important to keep investing and to prevent it becoming a slog.

***Here's an idea from me to you:***

Write snail mail to your friends. Write old-fashioned, paper letters to your friends. Decorate the envelope and make it yours. Make it personal, because what are friends for, if not for being personal?

By communicating on a different wavelength, by having slower and more intentional conversations, you will talk about deeper things with your friends and probably find much more appreciation for each other.

### Who should you send letters to?

This is something that is only really meaningful with close friends--those you share history with. Those you already know pretty well. Don't send these to shallow contacts or new friends (unless you and your recipient are both funky like that).

### What should you write about?

If it's your first time and you're not really sure what to write, here are a few suggestions:

Common prompts:

- What reminded you of your friend lately?
- Write down your favorite shared memory (in vivid detail!)
- Write about what you admire in them or what you are thankful for. Is there anything they have done recently, or in the past, that you are grateful for?
- What do you love and miss most about your friend?
- Write about the very first time you met your friend.
- Reminisce about special past experiences.

Other prompts:

- Discuss a completely outlandish hypothetical situation that would be the most fun with them: "If I had to spend 421 days on a deserted island, I'd choose you because..."
- Talk and brainstorm about a future event or trip you have planned or want to plan.

### Enhance the experience

- Choose high quality paper and envelopes to make the letter feel even more special.
- Add personal touches: include small drawings, stickers or pressed flowers to personalize the letter.
- Scented letters: lightly scent the letter with a favorite fragrance or essential oil for an extra sensory suprise
- link to a song you'd wish them to listen to
- Add a printed photo or Polaroid of the two of you, or a funny picture of yourself.

- Add some little gifts or pleasantries.
- Include parts of the text in ciphertext they have to decode.

### Delivery

Some fun ways of delivering your letter:

- Put it in a mailbox, duh;
- Hide it in between their stuff when they're not looking, like in the backpack, under a pillow, etc.
- Organise a little treasure hunt.

### Timeframe

When and how long between letters is something you may need to discuss with your recipient (or not at all if you are both that in tune). You can either do this in a normal conversation or set a baseline in your first letter.

It is important not to rush anything. Be carefull to leave room for ruminating, thought and reflection. I personally take at least three weeks to respond. Anything less and it feels rushed. Crafting a nice enveloppe and handwritten letter takes time. You can reply more quickly, but there should be a general consensus that a long time in between is perfectly OK. If you really want to get something off your chest, meet up and talk in real life.

### Tips

**Handwritting letters**: Writing the letter y hand makes it more personal and heartfelt. A handwritten letter stands out more than a printed A4 page.



**Keep it secret**: Keep the letters and their contents just between the two of you. While you may discuss the topics mentioned in the letters in real life, avoid talkung about the letters themselves or planning your next letter out loud. This keeps communication special and exclusive.
